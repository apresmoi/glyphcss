VoxCSS supports two projection modes that control how Z layers are spaced in the scene. The projection affects the overall feel of your voxel world without changing any voxel data.

## Cubic (default)

Each Z layer is spaced **50px** apart — the same as the voxel width and height. This gives a balanced, uniform isometric look where voxels appear as true cubes. Cubic is the default, so you don't need to set it explicitly. Every [Quickstart](/quickstart) example is cubic.

## Dimetric

Dimetric projection halves the Z spacing to **25px**, producing a flatter, more top-down perspective. Vertical height is visually compressed — good when your scene is about the ground plane more than the vertical axis.

## When to use dimetric

Dimetric works especially well for:

- **Terrain and landscapes** — flattened height gives a map-like overview.
- **Strategy game boards** — the top-down feel suits tile-based UIs.
- **Architectural floor plans** — emphasizes X/Y layout over vertical height.

If your scene is mostly about the ground plane and vertical detail is secondary, dimetric gives you a cleaner read of the spatial layout.
